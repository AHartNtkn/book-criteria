### What to aim for:

- **Cliche Avoidance at Story Level**: Whether the story avoids exhausted premises, character types, and plot patterns that have been done to the point of creative depletion — whether the story-level choices show awareness of what has already been written many times before.
- **Conceptual Originality (Premise Freshness)**: Whether the story's central premise, situation, or "what if" offers something the reader hasn't encountered in that exact form before, or combines familiar elements in a genuinely surprising way.
- **Concrete vs. Abstract Description**: Whether description operates in concrete, sensory terms rather than abstract, conceptual language.
- **Genre-Flavored Non-Genre Story**: The story uses genre vocabulary, setting elements, and character types but does not deliver the genre's core emotional experience. At chapter_plan: whether individual chapters deliver genre-specific dramatic experiences. At premise_ideation: whether the story concept genuinely engages with genre conventions at a structural level, or merely borrows surface elements.
- **Originality of Execution**: Whether the story finds fresh, non-default ways to handle scenes, transitions, character introductions, dialogue, revelations, and other narrative mechanics — distinct from whether the premise itself is original.
- **Premise Clarity**: Whether the core story concept can be articulated in a single, clear sentence that communicates protagonist, goal, and central conflict.
- **Specificity and Concrete Detail**: Whether the prose traffics in specific, concrete, sensory details that create vivid mental images — or in abstract, vague, conceptual language that fails to engage the reader's senses and imagination.
- **Thematic Premise as Dramatic Argument (Egri/Truby)**: Whether the story's thematic content functions as a dramatic argument with a clear premise that drives character and conflict.
- **Thematic Unity Across Story Elements**: Whether all major story elements (setting, dialogue, imagery, structure, character, plot) work in concert to express the theme, achieving organic unity.
- **Moral and Thematic Complexity**: Whether the thematic argument presents genuine complexity with competing valid perspectives, rather than a one-sided moral premise.
- **Character Self-Revelation**: Whether the narrative includes moments where characters discover truths about themselves that the reader has been prepared to understand, creating sudden emotional and thematic clarity.
- **Character-Driven Stakes**: Whether what's at risk in the story is rooted in what the character specifically and intensely cares about, creating personal investment that generates reader investment.
- **Curiosity Engine (Question Architecture)**: Whether the narrative actively generates reader curiosity by raising questions and strategically delaying answers, creating a forward-pulling engine of engagement.
- **Dramatic Irony**: Whether the text strategically places readers in a position of superior knowledge relative to characters, creating engagement through anticipation, cognitive dissonance, and emotional tension
- **Enter Late, Exit Early**: Whether scenes begin as close to the conflict/action as possible and end immediately after the core dramatic purpose is achieved, cutting unnecessary preamble and aftermath.
- **Flat vs Round Character Deployment**: Whether the author makes deliberate, effective choices about which characters receive full psychological development (round) versus serving as functional types (flat), deploying each appropriately.
- **Non-Default Narrative Choices**: Whether the writer makes deliberate, surprising choices at each decision point in the narrative (structure, POV, scene selection, character actions, resolutions) rather than defaulting to the most statistically common or expected option.
- **Relationship Dynamics**: Whether character relationships are varied, evolving, and revelatory -- each relationship drawing out different facets of the characters involved.
- **Surprise and Misdirection**: Whether the text delivers plot developments that are simultaneously unexpected and retrospectively inevitable — surprising the reader while making them admire the craft of the setup
- **Symbolism and Image System Effectiveness**: Whether the work employs symbols and image systems that reinforce theme through concrete imagery operating below conscious awareness.
- **Interpretive Richness and Ambiguity**: Whether the work supports multiple valid interpretations and rewards re-reading with new discoveries of meaning.
- **Meaning Inseparable from Form**: Whether the story's meaning can only be experienced through the story itself, rather than being reducible to a paraphrase or moral.
- **Reader Agency in Meaning-Making**: Whether the work invites active reader participation in constructing meaning, rather than delivering meaning passively.
- **Aggregate Consistency Across Length**: Whether consistency is maintained not just locally but across the full span of a novel-length or series-length work, where details introduced early must remain coherent with events hundreds of pages later.

### What to avoid:

- **Abstraction Trap Sentinel**: The text uses a high ratio of abstract conceptual vocabulary to concrete, tangible terms. At chapter_plan: scene descriptions use abstract conceptual vocabulary rather than concrete descriptions of what happens. At novel_plan: chapter summaries use abstract conceptual language rather than concrete descriptions of dramatic events.
- **Circling Conflicts**: The same argument, confrontation, or obstacle type recurs multiple times without meaningful change.
- **Exposition Front-Loading**: The first 10-20% consists primarily of exposition rather than dramatic action.
- **Grandiose Stakes Inflation**: Inflating mundane events to world-historical significance.
- **Metaphor Pile-Up Without Coherent Vehicle**: Multiple extended metaphors for the same concept from incompatible source domains.
- **Premise Inexpressible in One Sentence**: After reading the complete work, it is impossible to express the premise in a single sentence.
- **Premise Stated But Never Explored**: The story's core premise is established but the narrative never meaningfully explores it.
- **Repetitive Scene Beat Pattern**: Same sequence of beats across 5+ scenes.
- **S58: "Eyeball Kicks" — Stacked Nonsensical Metaphors**: Multiple stacked metaphors creating contradictory imagery.
- **Scenes Opening with Exposition Dumps**: Scenes consistently beginning with exposition before drama.
- **Sensory Detail Absence or Overload**: Either no sensory detail or excessive implausible sensory metaphors.
- **Subversion Without Foundation (Bait-and-Switch)**: Story dramatically shifting genre/tone/conflict contrary to opening promises.
- **Immediate Forgiveness After Betrayal (sentinel)**: A character betrays, harms, or deeply wrongs another character, and the victim forgives or reconciles with the betrayer within the same scene or the next scene without a process of rebuilding trust.
- **Sycophantic Resolution (sentinel)**: Every conflict thread is resolved, every character gets what they need, loose ends are tied up, and the ending produces complete closure with no lingering questions, costs, or ambiguity.
- **The Monoculture Alien (sentinel)**: Every member of a non-human race or foreign culture shares identical personality traits, values, and behaviors (all dwarves are gruff miners, all elves are wise and ancient, all orcs are violent).
