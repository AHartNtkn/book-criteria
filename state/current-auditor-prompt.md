## Criteria

### NC-004: Circular/Chiastic Structural Resonance
**What it measures**: Whether the story's ending echoes or mirrors its beginning in a way that creates structural satisfaction
**Score 0**: No structural relationship between beginning and ending; the story arrives at its conclusion without any echo of where it started; opportunities for meaningful mirroring are missed; or the story ends exactly where it began with no sense of change
**Score 5**: The ending mirrors the beginning through deliberate structural choices (returning to opening imagery, revisiting opening situations with transformed understanding, completing a thematic circuit); if chiastic structure is employed, mirrored elements create insight through both comparison and contrast; the final image meaningfully reflects or inverts the opening image; the reader experiences "instinctual satisfaction" from the structural symmetry

### NC-007: Dan Harmon Story Circle Completeness
**What it measures**: Whether the story completes the full circular journey of departure, transformation, and return
**Score 0**: The protagonist never leaves their comfort zone; or they leave but the return doesn't reflect transformation; the circular structure is incomplete — departure without return or return without change; the character's journey has no clear "low point" at the bottom of the circle
**Score 5**: All eight steps are present: (1) You — character in comfort zone, (2) Need — something is missing, (3) Go — entering unfamiliar situation, (4) Search — adapting to new world, (5) Find — getting what they wanted, (6) Take — paying a heavy price, (7) Return — going back to familiar situation, (8) Change — having been fundamentally altered; the low point occurs at the circle's bottom; the character's final state meaningfully contrasts their initial state

### NC-008: Denouement Proportionality
**What it measures**: Whether the post-climax resolution is appropriately proportioned — long enough to provide satisfaction but short enough to maintain energy
**Score 0**: The story ends immediately after the climax with no falling action, leaving readers disoriented; or the denouement drags on for many chapters after the central tension has resolved, losing reader interest; loose ends are either ignored or addressed at tedious length; the denouement re-introduces new conflicts
**Score 5**: The denouement occupies approximately 2-3% of the story (1-3 chapters for a novel); remaining conflict and tension are addressed and dissipated; important loose ends are tied up; character change is validated; the tone shifts from tension to resolution without abruptness; the ending earns its emotional weight without overstaying

### NC-010: Dramatica Completeness of Argument
**What it measures**: Whether the story investigates all significant approaches to its central problem
**Score 0**: The story presents only one approach to its central problem; alternative perspectives are strawmanned or absent; the story's "argument" is one-sided; the resolution feels predetermined rather than earned through exploration; the story mind is incomplete
**Score 5**: The story functions as a "Grand Argument Story" that investigates all significant approaches to its central problem; the protagonist's linear or holistic problem-solving style is challenged; multiple throughlines (main character, obstacle character, relationship, overall story) are present; the resolution provides "the most appropriate solution" because all alternatives have been explored and found wanting

### NC-018: Novel Length Appropriateness
**What it measures**: Whether the story's length is appropriate for its genre and content
**Score 0**: The story is dramatically shorter or longer than genre expectations without justification; the narrative is padded to reach a target length; or the narrative is compressed to the point where necessary development is missing; the length doesn't serve the story's structural needs
**Score 5**: The story's length falls within genre expectations (romance 65-80K, thriller 70-100K, fantasy 90-120K+, literary 80-100K, YA 50-80K) or deviates with clear justification; every chapter earns its length; the story doesn't feel padded or rushed; structural beats have appropriate breathing room; the pacing matches reader expectations for the genre

### NC-019: Organic Unity of Structure
**What it measures**: Whether all structural elements are interdependent and necessary, forming a cohesive whole
**Score 0**: Structural elements feel arbitrary or interchangeable; scenes, chapters, or sections could be rearranged without impact; the form doesn't serve the content; structural choices follow convention rather than the story's needs; removing or rearranging sections wouldn't affect the reader's experience
**Score 5**: Every structural element is necessary and interdependent; the "transposal or withdrawal of any one of them would disjoin and dislocate the whole" (Aristotle); the chosen structure is the only structure that could tell this particular story this particular way; form enhances content at every level; the story functions holistically rather than as a collection of independent parts

### NC-027: Redundancy and Structural Efficiency
**What it measures**: Whether the story avoids repeating the same structural beat or delivering the same information multiple times
**Score 0**: Multiple scenes pursue the same goal with the same type of obstacle; information is delivered multiple times through different characters or scenes; chapters chase the same outcome; emotional beats repeat without escalation; the story could be significantly compressed without losing anything
**Score 5**: Every scene has a unique five-word purpose statement; no two scenes repeat the same structural function; when similar situations recur, they escalate or transform; redundant beats have been merged or cut; the story achieves its narrative goals with maximum efficiency

### NC-028: Save the Cat Beat Completeness
**What it measures**: Whether the story contains all fifteen structural beats of the Save the Cat framework
**Score 0**: Fewer than half the fifteen beats are identifiable; the A Story (plot) and B Story (theme/relationship) never connect; no identifiable Fun and Games section delivering on the premise; no Dark Night of the Soul creating the low point before the finale; the Finale lacks the five sub-components
**Score 5**: All fifteen beats are present and functional: Opening Image (0-1%), Theme Stated (5%), Setup (1-10%), Catalyst (10%), Debate (10-20%), Break Into Two (20%), B Story (22%), Fun and Games (20-50%), Midpoint (50%, false victory or defeat), Bad Guys Close In (50-75%), All Is Lost (75%), Dark Night of the Soul (75-80%), Break Into Three (80%), Finale (80-99%, with five sub-components: Gathering the Team, Executing the Plan, High Tower Surprise, Dig Deep Down, Execution of New Plan), Final Image (99-100%)

### NC-030: Seven-Point Symmetry
**What it measures**: Whether the story's structure exhibits the symmetrical hook-resolution, turn-turn, pinch-pinch architecture
**Score 0**: No identifiable hook or resolution; no symmetrical relationship between the story's halves; pinch points are absent or don't escalate; the midpoint doesn't mark a shift from reaction to action; the structure is asymmetric without intentional purpose
**Score 5**: Seven points are present and symmetrical: Hook and Resolution are polar opposites; Plot Turn 1 and Plot Turn 2 mirror each other (entering the conflict / getting the final piece needed); Pinch 1 and Pinch 2 escalate (pressure / dire pressure); the Midpoint sits at the center where the protagonist shifts from reaction to action; the overall structure creates balance between the story's halves

### NC-031: Structural Approach Appropriateness
**What it measures**: Whether the chosen structural model (three-act, five-act, circular, kishotenketsu, etc.) is appropriate for the story being told
**Score 0**: A rigid structural template has been applied regardless of whether it fits; the story contorts to hit arbitrary beats; the structure fights against the story's natural shape; alternatively, no recognizable structure exists at all and the story wanders without architecture
**Score 5**: The structural model is chosen (or emergent) based on the story's needs; the structure enhances rather than constrains the content; if the story follows a recognizable template, it does so because the template fits, not because the template was imposed; structural innovation is present where convention would be limiting; the reader can feel the architecture without being distracted by it

### NC-035: Three-Act Proportional Balance
**What it measures**: Whether the novel's act structure follows sound proportional distribution (approximately 25% setup, 50% confrontation, 25% resolution)
**Score 0**: No discernible act structure; story weight distributed randomly; setup may consume 50% or resolution may be crammed into 5%; no identifiable structural movement from setup through confrontation to resolution
**Score 5**: Each act receives appropriate proportional weight; setup efficiently establishes characters and conflict without overstaying; the middle section has sufficient space for complication and development; resolution is neither rushed nor padded; structural transitions between acts are clear and motivated

## Sentinels

### NS-021: Three-Act Structure Applied as Rigid Template
**Detection**: The story rigidly follows a named structural template (Hero's Journey, Save the Cat, etc.) with every beat mechanically hit at exactly the prescribed percentage, regardless of whether the story's content calls for those specific beats
**Why it indicates autocomplete**: LLMs that have been trained on structural writing advice can reproduce template beats mechanically. The result is a story that "feels like it was written by an algorithm" (as critics of formulaic Hero's Journey applications note). The structural template is followed but the story within it doesn't require those specific turns at those specific moments.
